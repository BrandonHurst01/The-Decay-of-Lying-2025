Now, do you really imagine that the Japanese people, as we know them through the medium of as they are presented to us in 125art, have any existence? If you do, you have never understood Japanese art at all. The Japanese people are the deliberate invention self-conscious creation of certain individual artists.126 If you take a photograph of a Japanese gentleman or a gentl Japanese lady and set it beside a picture by Hokusai, or Hokkei, or any of the great Japanese artists, you will see that there is not the slightest resemblance between them. If you set a picture by Hokusia, or Hokkei, or any of the great native painters, beside a real Japanese gentleman or lady, or beside a photograph of a Japanese gentleman or lady, you will see that there is not the slightest resemblance between them. The actual people who live in Japan are not unlike the general run of English people;, that is to say, they are extremely commonplace, and without anything wonderful, or strange, or imaginative have nothing curious, or extraordinary about them. In fact, the whole of Japan is a pure invention. There is no such country, there are no such people. One of our most charming painters, whose tiny full-length portaits ofchildren are so beautiful and so powerful that he should be called the Velasquez to the Court of Lilliput went recently to the Japan Land of the Chrysanthemum in the foolish hopes of seeing the Japanese. All he saw, all he had the chance of painting, were a few lanterns and some fans. He was quite unable to discover the inhabitants, as anyone could see who visits his delightful exhibition at Messrs. Dowdeswell's Gallery showed only too well. He did not know that the Japanese people are, as I have said, simply a mode of style, a whimsical n exquisite fancy of art.127 And so, if you desire to see a Japanese effect, you will not behave like a tourist and go to Tokio. On the contrary, you will stay at home, and steep yourself in the work of certain Japanese artists, and then, when you have absorbed the spirit of their style, and caught their imaginative manner of vision, you will go some afternoon and sit in the Park, or stroll down Piccadilly, and if you cannot see an absolutely Japanese effect there, you will not see it anywhere.128
