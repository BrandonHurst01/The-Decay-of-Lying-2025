We should, at any rate, have no preferences, no prejudices, no partizan feeling of any kind. It is exactly because Hecuba is nothing to us that her sorrows are such an admirable motive for a tragedy. I do not know anything in the whole history of literature sadder than the artistic career of Charles Reade. He wrote one beautiful book, "The Cloister and the Hearth," a book as much above "Romola" as "Romola" is above 'Daniel Deronda", and the wasted the rest of his life in a foolish attempt to be modern, to draw public attention to the state of our convict prisons, and the management of our private lunatic asylums. Charles Dickens was bad depressing enough in all conscience when he tried to arouse our sympathy for the victims of the poor-law administration;, but Charles Reade, an artist, a scholar, a man with a true sense of beauty, raging and roaring over the abuses of modern contemporary life like a common pamphleteer, or a sensational journalist, is really a sight for the angels to weep over.
