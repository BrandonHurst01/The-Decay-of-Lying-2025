EgoismEgotism itself, which is so essential necessary to the a proper sense of human dignity, is absolutely the result of indoor life. Out of doors one becomes abstract and impersonal. One's individuality absolutely leaves one. And then nature is so indifferent, so unappreciative. Whenever I am walking in the park here, I always feel that I am no more to natureher than the cattle that browse on the slope, or the burdock that blooms in the ditch. Nothing is more evident than that Nature hates mind. Thinking is the most unhealthy thing in the world, and people die of it just as of any other disease. Fortunately, in England at least, it is not catching. Our splendid physique as a nation people is entirely due to our national stupidity.
