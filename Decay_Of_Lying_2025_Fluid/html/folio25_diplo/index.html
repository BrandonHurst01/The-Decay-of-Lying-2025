Wherever the former has been paramount, as in Byzantium Sicily and Spain by actual contact, or in the rest of Europe by the influence of the Crusades, we have had beautiful and imaginative work in which the visible things of life are transmuted into artistic convention, and the things that life has not are invented and fashioned for her delight. But wherever we have we have returned to life and nature our work has always become vulgar, common, and uninteresting.
