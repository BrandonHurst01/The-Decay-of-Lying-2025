It is the ages that are her symbols, her reflections, her echoes. Even those who hold that Art is representative of time, and place, and people, who cannot help admitting that the the more imitative an art is, the less representative it represents to us the spirit of its time age. The evil faces of the Roman Eemperors look at out at us from the foul porphyry and spotted jasper in which the ideal or realistic artists of the day delighted to work, and we fancy that in those cruel lips and heavy sensual jaws we can find the secret of the ruin of the Empire. Butit was not so. Neither The vices of Tiberius could not destroy that supreme civilization, any more than the virtues of the Antonines could save it. It fell for other, for greater reasons.
